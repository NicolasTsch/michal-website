Michal Oren, named a Classic FM 2026 Rising Star, is a conductor whose work is shaped by a deep sense of narrative and musical imagination. Originally from Tel-Aviv, Israel, she was recently announced as the next Calleva Assistant Conductor of the Bournemouth Symphony Orchestra (2026–2027). She is also one of six semi-finalists in the upcoming 80th Concours de Genève Conducting Competition in November 2026.

Recent Seasons

Recently she was selected to the Taki Alsop Conducting Fellowship Mentoring Programme and is invited to the renowned Gstaad Conducting Academy in summer 2026, where she will receive lessons from Jaap van Zweden, Johannes Schlaefli and Alexander Shelley. Previously, Michal held the Female Conductor Trainee position at Opera North in Leeds, UK (2024–2025).

Awards & Recognition

Michal won 1st prize in the 4th International Academy and Competition of Orchestra Conducting in Estoril, Portugal (2023) and 2nd prizes in the 5th Koussevitzky Conducting Competition in Grosseto, Italy (2025) and in the 3rd International Orchestral Conducting Competition "Universidad de Almería", Spain (2023). In 2024, she joined the two-year Women Conductors' Programme of the Royal Philharmonic Society with the Royal Northern Sinfonia, led by Alice Farnham, and participated in the Young Conductors Showcase with the BBC Scottish Symphony Orchestra in Glasgow. Michal was the Residence Music Scholar of the Robert Anderson Trust (2022–2025), and since 2015 is a long-standing scholar of the America-Israel Cultural Foundation.

Education & Guidance

Michal completed her Professional Diploma in Conducting at the Royal Academy of Music in London with a full scholarship under conductor Sian Edwards, holding the Bianca Falcone Sorrell Award. Previously, she earned her Master's Degree in Orchestral Conducting from the Royal College of Music, supported by a full scholarship as a Victor and Lilian Hochhauser Scholar. She holds two Bachelors of Music in orchestral conducting and clarinet performance from the Buchmann-Mehta School of Music at Tel Aviv University.

Among Michal's teachers are the conductors Sian Edwards, Martyn Brabbins, Toby Purser, Peter Stark, Howard Williams, Yi-An Xu, Doron Salomon, and IPO principal clarinettist Yevgeny Yehudin.

In addition, Michal participated in masterclasses of world-renowned conductors such as Daniele Gatti, Andrew Davis, Vasily Petrenko, Sakari Oramo, Nicolas Pasquet, Mark Stringer, Pascal Rophé and Lahav Shani.

Artistic Vision

Alongside her conducting work, Michal is the founder of the Museum Orchestra in collaboration with the Petach Tikva Museum of Art. The orchestra performs a repertoire that offers a musical interpretation of visual arts exhibits. This project reflects her ongoing exploration of music as a form of storytelling, creating performances that respond and reimagine visual art through sound.
